Module: Research Methods

Posted on May 1, 2023 by DM
Risk Analyst Post-Graduate

Unit 1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics

The benefits of using Inductive and deductive reasoning were discussed in detail. Figure 1 - Types of reasoning (Source: Cherednichenko, 2020) The differences between Ethos, Pathos and Logos were examined. This led to the understanding of objecitive and subjective reasoning. The usefulness of ethics as a part of the scientific method was also analyzed.

Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

My personal topic for the Literature Review, was the Frankfurt Deutschebahn train system. I found it fascinating that they are migrating to the cloud across the majority of their departments. The conclusions from my literature review made this topic somewhat impractical for the formulation of my research proposal however.

Unit 3: Research Methods

Quantitative and Qualitative methodologies were investigated in detail. Qualitative studies involve interviews, while quantitative research involves experiments and questionnaires. The seminar involved exploring these research methods.

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Various qualitative research methods were investigated in-depth. The one I personally found the most interesting and useful was focus groups. There is a deep, sociological aspect, almost tantamount to profiling, which I found quite intriguing. This is especially fascinating as to how it relates with statistical models (Kitzinger, 1995).

Unit 5: Interviews and Survey Methods

Of great benefit was the explanation of how to conduct a proper interview or survey template. Effort should always be placed on emphasizing both the Objective and Subjective nature of enquiry (Fowler, 2013). • Personal Interviews provide Body Language, which may imply accentuation to their statements. • Subject Matter Expert Interviews provide a greater level of technical information.

Unit 6: Questionnaire Design

Methods for Data Collection

Unit 7: Validity and Generalisability in Research

The topic mostly revolved around reflective thinking. In unit 7 a collaborative learning discussion was conducted. I responded to three of my peers, and enquired deeper about ethical considerations.

Unit 8: Inferential Statistics

In this module, we learnt about employing the Mean, Median and Standard Deviation functions. This are useful when trying to categorize data (Berenson, 2015). I acquired invaluable skills regarding statistical inferencing. I took the course testcases and implemented them in Python.

Unit 9: Analysing Qualitative Data

In Unit 9, we had some more interesting statistical analyses: I enjoyed implementing data analysis on them. As per usual, I utilized Python for data modelling.

Unit 10: Research Writing

I completed my research proposal outline and presentation. I attempted to linearise my thoughts and holisticly convey my ideas in my videologue, but I was quite nervous. I maintained good academic form, structured my presentation in line with industry norms. My outline was rather neat and concise.

Unit 12: Project Management and Managing Risk

Project management techniques such as the STRIDE and SPIRAL methodologies were examined in detail. I found STRIDE to be particularly beneficial (Hijazi, 2012).

Conclusion - Review of Course as a Whole

This Module has provided me with the very necessary pre-requisites to tackle a Master’s Thesis. I feel confident in peer-reviewing research articles, and my knowledge of statistics has expanded. I’m grateful to the course curators and designers for this well thought out curriculum.

References
