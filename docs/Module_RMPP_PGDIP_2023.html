Module: Research Methods and Professional Practice

Posted on May 1, 2023 by DM
Risk Analyst Post-Graduate

Introduction

Reading Material

Unit 1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics

The benefits of using Inductive and deductive reasoning were discussed in detail. Figure 1 - Types of reasoning (Source: Cherednichenko, 2020) The differences between Ethos, Pathos and Logos were examined. This led to the understanding of objecitive and subjective reasoning. The usefulness of ethics as a part of the scientific method was also analyzed.

Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

My personal topic for the Literature Review, was the Frankfurt Deutschebahn train system. I found it fascinating that they are migrating to the cloud across the majority of their departments. The conclusions from my literature review made this topic somewhat impractical for the formulation of my research proposal however. Figure 2 – Slide from Seminar 2 – Unit 3

Unit 3: Research Methods

Quantitative and Qualitative methodologies were investigated in detail. Qualitative studies involve interviews, while quantitative research involves experiments and questionnaires. The seminar involved exploring these research methods.

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Various qualitative research methods were investigated in-depth. The one I personally found the most interesting and useful was focus groups. There is a deep, sociological aspect, almost tantamount to profiling, which I found quite intriguing. This is especially fascinating as to how it relates with statistical models (Kitzinger, 1995).

Unit 5: Interviews and Survey Methods

Of great benefit was the explanation of how to conduct a proper interview or survey template. Effort should always be placed on emphasizing both the Objective and Subjective nature of enquiry (Fowler, 2013). • Personal Interviews provide Body Language, which may imply accentuation to their statements. • Subject Matter Expert Interviews provide a greater level of technical information.

Unit 6: Questionnaire Design

Methods for Data Collection Figure 3 – Survey Design

Unit 7: Validity and Generalisability in Research

The topic mostly revolved around reflective thinking. In unit 7 a collaborative learning discussion was conducted. I responded to three of my peers, and enquired deeper about ethical considerations.

Figure 4 – Initial Post Figure 5 – Peer Response 1 Figure 6 – Peer Response 2 Figure 7 – Peer Response 3

Unit 8: Inferential Statistics

In this module, we learnt about employing the Mean, Median and Standard Deviation functions. This are useful when trying to categorize data (Berenson, 2015). I acquired invaluable skills regarding statistical inferencing. I took the course testcases and implemented them in Python.

Figure 8 – Excel Workbook Exa 8.6B on Weightloss Table 1 – Useful Excel spreadsheet functions Figure 9 – Defining a data range in Excel Table 2 – Statistical formulas in algebraic format E-Portfolio Research Methods & Professional Practice 13 Figure 10 – An example of an exercise I performed from the workbook Figure 11 – My python implementation of the course’s Exa 8.6B exercise Figure 12 – The results of my Python statistical inferencing code

Unit 9: Analysing Qualitative Data

In Unit 9, we had some more interesting statistical analyses: I enjoyed implementing data analysis on the courses spreadsheets. As per usual, I utilized Python for data modelling. Figure 13 – Graph detailing Python output from Workbook Exe 9

Unit 10: Research Writing

I completed my research proposal outline and presentation. I attempted to linearise my thoughts and holisticly convey my ideas in my videologue, but I was quite nervous. I maintained good academic form, structured my presentation in line with industry norms. My outline was rather neat and concise.

Unit 11: Professional Development – Your e-Portfolio

In this Unit we read about the importance of e-portfolios. Authors such as Winchell (2018) explain the significance of this in great detail. The general consensus is that a well designed e-portfolio can help candidates achieve career goals and gain employment. A great deal of e-portfolio work is web design related, and as such I do need to develop my skills a bit further. Within this unit, we delved into reflective writing. Many universities have important articles on this topic, such as the University of Birmingham (2015) and the University of Portsmouth (2015).

Unit 12: Project Management and Managing Risk

Project management techniques such as the STRIDE and SPIRAL methodologies were examined in detail. I found STRIDE to be particularly beneficial (Hijazi, 2012). Figure 15 – Spiral Risk Methodology (Source: Gurendo, 2015)

Assessments

Summative Assignment 2 – Literature Review

I chose to explore Frankfurt’s Railway System. [Reflections]: I feel I could have done a better job, had I been more open to more experimental research methodologies (Fry,2007; Phelps, 2018; Otten, 2015). Also, I should have been more relaxed

Conclusion - Review of Course as a Whole

I greatly benefited from the statistical work in this module. It will be of immense use in my Master’s Thesis. I am already planning on different research models for my surveys and interviews. I have learnt new research methodologies that I can put into practice and I feel this module will help me with my academic career ahead. This Module has provided me with the very necessary pre-requisites to tackle a Master’s Thesis. I feel confident in peer-reviewing research articles, and my knowledge of statistics has expanded. I am grateful to the course curators and designers for this well thought out curriculum.
