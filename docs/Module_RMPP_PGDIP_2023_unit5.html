Unit 5: Interviews and Survey Methods

This module examines the professional way of conducting interviews in a research setting.

According to Dawson (2015), interviews and surveys are a necessary part of any research project. Academic sources like Fowler (2013) emphasized the rationality of both the Objective and Subjective natures of any enquiry or research, and that misinterpreting their differences can lead to incorrect conclusions.

For deeper analysis of statistical bias and accurate random sampling, Campos (2015) provided quite a bit of insight.

According to Meyer's (2023) perspective on interviews:

  • Personal Interviews provide aspects of Body Language, which may imply accentuation or alternatives to their statements.
  • Subject Matter Expert Interviews provide a greater level of technical information.
The lecturecast primarily examined Interview and Survey Design, as well as their statistical utility as exmined by Berenson (2015).

References

  • Berenson, L., Levine, D. & Szabat, K. (2015) Basic Business Statistics: Concepts and Applications - Chapter 7. 13th Ed. Pearson.

  • Meyer, S., Tyger, C., Garrison, G. & Rohrbacher, A. (2023) Stress Effects During In-Person and Online Simulated Interviews.
